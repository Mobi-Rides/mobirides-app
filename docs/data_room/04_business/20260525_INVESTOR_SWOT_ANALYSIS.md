# MobiRides Investor SWOT Analysis

**Date:** May 25, 2026  
**Status:** Launch-ready investor reference  
**Related issue:** MOB-128 / AUD-005

## Strengths

- First-mover positioning in Botswana peer-to-peer car sharing with marketplace and Rent2Buy expansion paths.
- Production-grade core platform with verified booking, handover, wallet, legal-consent, and admin workflows.
- Differentiated trust stack: KYC, damage protection terms, signed rental agreements, and admin audit trails.
- Active strategic materials for Pay-U damage protection, fleet partnerships, and payment-provider integration.

## Weaknesses

- Real-money payment provider integration remains **Mock/Pending** until DPO/PayGate/Ooze credentials, sandbox tests, webhooks, and payout rails are complete.
- Investor data room still requires formal corporate records such as final cap table, signed shareholder documents, and bank statements for full diligence.
- Test coverage and regression automation need continued uplift before scale marketing.

## Opportunities

- Botswana urban mobility gap and informal rental behavior create room for a trusted digital marketplace.
- Host income pressure and vehicle underutilization support strong supply-side acquisition messaging.
- Regional expansion options in SADC markets can be staged after Botswana launch validation.
- Rent2Buy pilot can expand the platform from short-term rental into ownership pathways.

## Threats

- Payment credential delays could constrain public launch readiness and conversion.
- Larger regional rental or ride-hailing platforms could move into P2P supply aggregation.
- Insurance, KYC, and data-protection compliance must remain current as transaction volume grows.
- Any unresolved launch regressions in booking/payment/handover flows could weaken investor and user confidence.

## Investor-Ready Position

MobiRides is launch-ready for investor review as a pre-revenue / early-commercialization opportunity, provided the payment state is presented accurately as **Mock/Pending** and the remaining diligence artifacts are handled as closing conditions.
